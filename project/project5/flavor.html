Global Cuisines

While you may be a busy student eating on a budget, that doesn’t mean you can’t expand your palate at the same time! Each global cuisine has its own unique flavor profile, and some essential spices/ ingredients. Use this guide as a reference for your shopping list, whether you’re sticking to a lifelong favorite, or trying something new!

Chinese

Flavors: Savory, umami, sweet, spicy Ingredients: Soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, chili paste, scallions, star anise, five-spice powder, Sichuan peppercorns Tips: Combine soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger for a quick stir-fry sauce Recipe Ideas: vegetable fried rice, egg drop soup, sweet and sour chicken, garlic stir fry noodles, Mapo tofu

Italian

Flavors: Herbs, garlic, olive oil, tangy, and cheesy Ingredients: Olive oil, garlic, basil, oregano, tomatoes (fresh or canned), Parmesan, balsamic vinegar, mozzarella, Italian seasoning, red pepper flakes Tips: Toss cooked pasta with olive oil, garlic, and Parmesan for a quick Italian-inspired meal Recipe Ideas: olive oil and garlic pasta, Margherita pizza, caprese panini, pesto pasta, minestrone soup

Greek

Flavors: Bright, herbaceous, tangy, citrusy and earthy Ingredients: Olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, dill, feta cheese, yogurt, cucumbers, mint, thyme Tips: Marinate chicken in olive oil, lemon, garlic, and oregano for a simple Greek-inspired dish Recipe Ideas: Greek salad, chicken souvlaki wraps, hummus and pita, spanakopita, falafel, tabbouleh

Mexican

Flavors: Spicy, smoky, zesty, and savory Ingredients: Chili powder, cumin, lime, garlic, onion, tomatoes, cilantro, avocados, corn, black beans, paprika, chipotle, cayenne Tips: Use cumin, chili powder, and lime to season beans or ground meat for tacos Recipe Ideas: bean and cheese burritos, chicken quesadillas, beef tacos, loaded nachos, enchiladas, empanadas

Indian

Flavors: Spicy, aromatic, earthy, and creamy. Ingredients: Turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, garlic, yogurt, coconut milk, tomatoes, curry powder, garam masala, mustard seeds Tips: Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger with curry powder for a quick curry base Recipe Ideas: chana masala, lentil soup (dal), chicken tikka masala, vegetable biryani, aloo gobi

Japanese

Flavors: Umami, clean, slightly sweet, and mild Ingredients: Soy sauce, miso, mirin, sake, dashi (broth), seaweed, sesame seeds, wasabi, togarashi (chili blend), pickled ginger Tips: Combine soy sauce, sugar, and a little sesame oil for a quick teriyaki-style sauce Recipe Ideas: teriyaki chicken, miso soup, veggie sushi rolls, ramen noodles, gyoza

Thai

Flavors: Sweet, sour, salty, and spicy Ingredients: Lime, coconut milk, fish sauce, garlic, ginger, lemongrass, Thai chilies, peanuts, curry paste, cilantro, basil Tips: Stir fry vegetables in coconut milk, curry paste, and lime for a quick Thai-inspired dish Recipe Ideas: pad Thai, green curry with tofu, Thai basil chicken, tom yum soup, mango sticky rice

Korean

Flavors: Spicy, sweet, umami, and fermented Ingredients: Gochujang (chili paste), soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, rice vinegar, kimchi, sesame seeds, scallions Tips: Mix gochujang with soy sauce and sesame oil for a quick Korean-style marinade Recipe Ideas: kimchi fried rice, bibimbap, Korean BBQ beef, spicy tofu stir fry, Korean pancakes, tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes)
